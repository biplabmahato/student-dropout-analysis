Insights from dropout data analysis can guide the development of policies, programs, and resource allocation decisions at the school, district, and state levels. Ultimately, the goal of student dropout analysis is to identify effective ways to keep more students engaged in their education, improve graduation rates, and enhance overall student outcomes.

The literacy rate of parents or guardians is closely linked to the educational attainment and support they can provide for their children. Students from families with lower literacy levels may have less academic guidance, resources, and encouragement to pursue and complete their education.

Teacher-pupil ratio, also known as the student-teacher ratio, refers to the average number of students per teacher in an educational institution or a school system. The teacher-pupil ratio is calculated by dividing the total number of students enrolled in a school or educational system by the total number of teachers employed.

The midday meal provides a nutritious and balanced meal to students, which helps address issues of hunger, malnutrition, and micronutrient deficiencies. Improved nutrition can enhance students' physical and cognitive development, leading to better learning outcomes.

Educating girls has a direct positive impact on a country's economic growth and development. Educated women are more likely to participate in the workforce, have higher incomes, and contribute to the overall productivity and prosperity of their communities.

Medical facilities within schools play a crucial role in supporting student health, wellbeing, and educational outcomes. This proactive approach helps maintain student health and reduces absenteeism due to preventable medical conditions.
